Water Cleanliness

Eutrophication is the excessive accumulation of nutrients in a water way (Panayides). [view why this happens] Common nutrients used for agricultural fertilizers are combinations of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. A 2025 study tested the effect of a common fertilizer, ammonium nitrate, on dissolved oxygen levels in a freshwater ecosystem. The study found that ammonium nitrate concentrations of 15 mg/L lowered dissolved oxygen significantly in a 500 mL environment over just a 4 week process (Panayides). Reduced dissolved oxygen levels can render an aquatic habitat inhabitable for many species. [view graph]
Traditional, largescale agriculture not only harms aquatic habitats, but can also harm human health. A 2025 research study analyzed the extent to which anthropogenic arsenic pollution impacted lung cancer rates. The study found that agricultural runoff and drainage of waterwells contributed to excess arsenic concentrations in water (Panayides). Arsenic ingested from water sources was found to increase rates of lung cancer (Panayides). [view how this works]
